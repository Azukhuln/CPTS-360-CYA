Reflection:

I am not currently working in a technical position, and have been struggling to secure myself such a position. For this CYA, I made it my goal to apply to as many summer internships I could, while engaging with networking and career-focused activities. Over spring break, I spent hours applying to as many relevant positions as I could find, with most of the time being spent on cover letters. I also went to networking events such as career expo’s, and AI networking events, and a presentation by INNL.

The main decisions I made were which positions to apply to. While my goal was to apply to as many internships as possible, this was tempered with the decision making of which positions I actually had a chance to hear back from. I mostly applied to software, data analytics  or web development internships, as I my qualifications matched these positions the most. 

Besides the sense of satisfaction, I am assessing my performance mainly through comparison with my baseline. I applied to more jobs and went to more events in the few weeks that this CYA has been active, than I have in the previous year. The other measure of success is whether or not the actual time and evidence requirements of the CYA are being met, and I can say that they have. 

If I am able to secure a tech internship, that will significantly affect my career goals. Internships look very good on a resume, and having professional experience will significantly improve my chances of securing a good job outside of college. Furthermore pursuing this CYA has given me significantly more experience with writing cover letters. Through this exploration, I found a few different websites for applying to internships, some of which were easier or more efficient to use than the methods I had been using in the past. Ultimately this experience has given me a lot more skill in applying to these kinds of positions, and has increased the likelihood that I will be able to get such a position in the future.
